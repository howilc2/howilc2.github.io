If you’re looking to install slip on rocker panels, you’ve come to the right place. This guide will take you step-by-step through the process to make it as easy as possible.

Repairing or replacing the rocker panels on your vehicle can be daunting, especially if you’re not experienced with auto body work. Not only can it be a tedious process, but it can also be costly if you choose to take it to a professional. However, the good news is that with a little bit of patience and the right tools, you can install slip on rocker panels yourself.

The first thing you need to do is gather all the necessary tools and equipment. You’ll need a drill, screwdriver, wheel chocks, and of course, the replacement rocker panels. Make sure you’ve also taken the time to prep your work area properly.

Now that you have all the necessary tools and equipment, it’s time to get down to business. Start by loosening the nuts and bolts that attach the old rocker panels to the vehicle. Once they’re removed, take the time to clean the area thoroughly to ensure a good fit for the new rocker panels.

Why and how to install slip on rocker panels

In my personal experience, slip on rocker panels are a great option for those who want to repair or replace the existing ones. They can easily be installed without welding or extensive bodywork, which makes them a popular choice for beginners.

First, start by aligning the new rocker panel with the bottom of the vehicle and use a drill to make holes for the screws. Then, you can fit the slip on rocker panel in place and start securing it with the screws. Make sure to tighten the screws and bolts to ensure a secure fit.

Tips for installing slip on rocker panels

Before you start installing the new rocker panels, make sure you’ve prepped the area properly. This includes cleaning and sanding any rust or damage around the affected area.

Another tip is to test fit the replacement panels before starting the installation process. This will give you an idea of how they should be aligned and ensure a proper fit. It’s also important to refer to the manufacturer’s instructions for any specific instructions or guidelines.

How to personalize your slip on rocker panels

If you’re looking to add a personal touch to your vehicle, you can easily do so with slip on rocker panels. There are various finishes and styles available, such as diamond plate, textured, or smooth. Consider picking a finish that matches your personal style or the overall look of your vehicle.

The benefits of installing slip on rocker panels

Aside from their ease of installation, slip on rocker panels also offer a few other benefits. They can help protect your vehicle from rust and corrosion caused by salt, water, and other elements. Additionally, they can add to the overall value and appearance of your vehicle.

Question and Answer

Q: Do I need to weld the slip on rocker panels in place?

A: No, slip on rocker panels do not require welding. They attach to the vehicle using screws and bolts.

Q: Do I need any special tools to install slip on rocker panels?

A: While having the proper tools is important, you don’t need any special tools to install slip on rocker panels. The basic tools, such as a drill, screwdriver, and wheel chocks, should suffice.

Q: How long does it take to install slip on rocker panels?

A: The installation process can vary depending on your level of expertise and the complexity of the job. However, on average, it can take anywhere from 2-4 hours.

Q: Can I use slip on rocker panels on any vehicle make and model?

A: Slip on rocker panels are designed to fit specific vehicle makes and models. Make sure to refer to the manufacturer’s instructions to ensure the proper fit.

Conclusion of how to install slip on rocker panels

Installing slip on rocker panels may seem daunting at first but with the proper tools and patience, it can be done by anyone. Don’t let the cost of professional installation keep you from protecting your vehicle and adding to its appearance. Remember to test fit the new rocker panels, clean the work area thoroughly, and use the manufacturer’s instructions as a guide. Happy installing!
