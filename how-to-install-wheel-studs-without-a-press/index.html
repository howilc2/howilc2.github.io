If you’re changing your Mustang wheels, you might find yourself in a bit of a bind. What do you do when your wheel studs are too short? You don’t have a press, so how do you install wheel studs without one?

The Problem

Installing wheel studs without a press can be a challenge. It’s a common issue for Mustang owners who are struggling to replace their wheels. The studs can often be too short, making it impossible to fit your new wheels. This can be a frustrating and daunting task that requires a bit of know-how.

The Solution

There are several ways to install wheel studs without a press. The first step is to remove the old studs. You can do this by carefully hammering them out with a punch. Once you’ve removed the old studs, you can insert the new ones.

Main Points

To install wheel studs without a press, you need to:

  • Remove the old studs with a punch and hammer
  • Clean and inspect the holes
  • Insert the new studs until they seat properly
  • Once the stud is seated, use a stud installer tool to press it in further

First Method: Drum Brake Equipped Cars

On drum brake equipped cars, the process is fairly simple. With a hammer and punch, you can drive the wheel stud out of its hole. Before installing the new stud, you should clean and inspect the area to ensure it is ready for the new stud. With the new stud in hand, it’s a matter of positioning it in the hole and tapping it home. Once the stud is in position, a stud installer tool can be used to press it home completely. By doing this, you ensure that the stud is properly seated and ready to hold your new wheel.

Second Method: Disc Brake Equipped Cars

On cars with disc brakes, the process is slightly more difficult. You must first remove the caliper, rotor, and hub to gain access to the stud. Once you have access, you can proceed with the same process as before: hammer the old stud out, clean and inspect the area, and tap the new stud home. Use a stud installer tool to press the stud home once it’s in the correct position.

Here are some tips to make the process easier:

  • Use a drill to enlarge the hole slightly if the new stud isn’t lining up
  • Make sure the threads are lubricated before installation
  • Double-check that the stud is in the correct position before pressing with the installer tool

Final Thoughts

Installing wheel studs without a press can be a bit of a challenge, but it’s doable. With the right tools and a little patience, you can replace your wheel studs and get your Mustang back on the road in no time.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I install new wheel studs without removing the hub?

A: No, you must remove the hub to install new studs.

Q: What’s the easiest way to remove the old stud?

A: Use a hammer and punch to drive it out of the hole.

Q: Can I use an impact wrench to install the wheel stud?

A: It’s recommended to use a stud installer tool for best results.

Q: Can I use this method on other vehicles besides Mustangs?

A: Yes, this method can be used on other vehicles as well. Just make sure you’re using the correct size and type of wheel stud for your vehicle.

Conclusion of how to install wheel studs without a press

If you’ve been struggling with how to install wheel studs without a press, hopefully, this guide has helped. By following these steps and using the right tools, you can replace your Mustang’s wheel studs with ease. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you’ll be back on the road in no time.
