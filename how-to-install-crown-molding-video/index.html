How to Install Crown Molding Video: Tips and Tricks

When it comes to installing crown molding, there are a few tips and tricks that can make the process go more smoothly. Here are a few of my favorites:

  • Use a coping saw to cut the molding for corners.
  • Always measure twice and cut once.
  • Use a nail gun to attach the molding to the wall.
  • Paint or stain the molding before installation to save time.

How to Cope the Corners

Coping the corners is one of the trickiest parts of installing crown molding. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Make a 45-degree angle cut on the end of the first piece of molding.
  2. Use a coping saw to cut along the profile of the molding at the end of the second piece.
  3. Fine-tune the coped end with a file or sandpaper until it fits snugly against the first piece.
  4. Attach the molded to the wall using a nail gun.

How to Hide Seams

One of the challenges of installing crown molding is hiding the seams where the pieces come together. Here’s how to do it:

  • Apply a small bead of caulk along the seams to fill in any gaps.
  • Smooth the caulk with your finger or a caulk smoothing tool.
  • Once the caulk is dry, paint or stain the molding.
