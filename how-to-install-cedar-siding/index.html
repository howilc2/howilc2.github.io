Are you thinking about installing cedar siding in your home? Cedar siding is not only beautiful but also provides excellent insulation and durability. However, installing cedar siding can be a daunting task, but with the right knowledge, the process can be rewarding. In this article, we will guide you through the steps on how to install cedar siding for your home.

Pain Points of Installing Cedar Siding

Installing cedar siding can be challenging, especially if you are a novice. Some pain points include making sure the siding aligns, choosing the right thickness of the cedar, and understanding what type of cedar siding will work best for your home.

How to Install Cedar Siding

Before starting the installation process, you need to gather the necessary tools and materials. You will need a saw, nails, hammer, level, and cedar siding, among other things.

The first step is to prepare the surface where you will be installing the cedar siding. Ensure that the wall is clean, dry, and free from any debris. Then, install the starter strip at the bottom of the wall and attach it using nails.

Next, install the corner posts, windows, and door trims. Make sure that they are plumb and level. Then, start installing the cedar siding by attaching the first board at the corner, ensuring that it is level and plumb. Continue installing the other boards, making sure that they align correctly.

When you reach the top of the wall, install the top trim board and finish with the top edge flashing. Finally, install the J-channel around the windows and doors to complete the installation process.

Summary

In summary, installing cedar siding involves preparing the surface, installing the starter strip, corner posts, windows, and door trims, attaching the cedar siding, and completing the process with J-channel. It is crucial to align the boards correctly and choose the right thickness of the cedar siding for your home.

Choosing the Right Cedar Siding

Personal Experience:

When choosing cedar siding, it is essential to consider the type of cedar that will complement your home’s architecture. There are various cedar siding styles such as beveled, tongue and groove, and board-and-batten. Beveled cedar siding, for instance, is ideal for modern and traditional homes.

When deciding which cedar siding to use, consider the climate of your area. If you live in a humid area, you may want to consider using thicker cedar siding to prevent rot and decay.

When it comes to the finish of the cedar siding, you can either choose to paint or stain the cedar. Painting gives you unlimited color options, while staining the cedar siding enhances the natural texture and color of the wood.

Maintaining Cedar Siding

Personal Experience:

Maintaining cedar siding is essential to ensure that it lasts for years and maintains its beauty. It is recommended that you wash the cedar siding every six months or so to remove dirt and debris. Additionally, ensure that you inspect the cedar siding regularly for any damage and make necessary repairs to prevent further damage.

When it comes to refinishing cedar siding, you should do it every six to eight years, depending on the climate of your area. Refinishing the cedar siding helps to protect it from rot and decay, ensuring that it lasts longer.

Installing Cedar Shingle Siding

Personal Experience:

Installing cedar shingle siding involves preparing the surface of the wall, installing the starter strip, and then attaching the cedar shingle siding. Cedar shingle siding is lightweight and easy to install, making it an ideal option for the DIY enthusiast.

When installing cedar shingle siding, ensure that it is installed at an angle of 45 degrees to prevent water from seeping through the cracks. Additionally, use a moisture barrier between the shingles and the wall to prevent water from rotting the wall.

Cedar Shake vs. Cedar Shingle Siding

Personal Experience:

Cedar shake siding is thicker and heavier than cedar shingle siding, making it durable and long-lasting. It also has a rougher texture and natural-looking finish. On the other hand, cedar shingle siding is thinner and lighter, making it easier to install. It has a smoother finish and is often painted or stained.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does cedar siding last?

A: With proper installation and maintenance, cedar siding can last up to 50 years or more.

Q: Can I install cedar siding myself?

A: Yes, you can install cedar siding yourself, but it is essential to have the necessary skills and equipment.

Q: Is cedar siding expensive?

A: Cedar siding is more expensive than vinyl siding but less expensive than other wood siding options such as redwood.

Q: Can you paint cedar siding?

A: Yes, you can paint cedar siding. However, it is essential to use a high-quality primer and paint to ensure that the paint adheres well.

Conclusion of How to Install Cedar Siding

Installing cedar siding takes time and effort, but with the right knowledge and tools, it can be a rewarding process. Choose the right thickness and style of cedar siding that complements your home’s architecture and climate. Remember to maintain and refinish the cedar siding regularly to ensure that it lasts for years to come. Happy Installing!
