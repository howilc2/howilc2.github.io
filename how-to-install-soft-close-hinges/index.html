Soft close hinges are a must-have addition to any kitchen or bathroom cabinet. They eliminate slamming while providing a gentle closing motion. If you’re tired of hearing that loud banging sound every time you shut a cabinet door, then you need to learn how to install soft close hinges. Not only will they save the structure of your cabinets, but they’ll also create a quieter and more peaceful environment.

Pain Points of How To Install Soft Close Hinges

Installing soft close hinges may seem like an overwhelming task, but it doesn’t have to be. The most significant challenge of installing these hinges is adjusting them correctly to make sure the cabinet doors close gently. However, with the right tools and some patience, anyone can install these hinges with ease.

Answering the Target of How To Install Soft Close Hinges

The following steps will guide you through the process of installing soft close hinges:

  1. Remove your old cabinet hinges
  2. Install the mounting plates and hinge cups on the cabinet and door
  3. Attach the soft close mechanism to the mounting plates
  4. Adjust the hinges until the door closes softly and securely

Be sure to refer to the manufacturer’s instructions for specific guidance on your hinges. Once you’ve installed your new hinges, enjoy the smooth and quiet operation they provide.

Reasons to Install Soft Close Hinges

Have you ever had a glass in your hand, and while closing a cabinet door with the other hand, the door slammed shut, causing you to spill your drink? Soft close hinges prevent this from happening. ### How to Install Soft Close Hinges- The Process

Firstly, you need to detach the old hinges from the cabinets, ensuring you keep all screws and attachments in a safe place. Secondly, you’ll need to pre-drill holes in the cabinet so that it’s easy to attach the mounting plates. Once the mounting plates are attached, you can attach the hinge cups on the doors. Screw the hinge cups in place, and then attach the soft close mechanism to the mounting plates. Adjust the hinges until they’re at the right angle, and the door closes softly and securely.
