How to Install Running Boards on Chevy Silverado

Before beginning your installation, you’ll need to gather all the tools and equipment you will need. This includes the running boards themselves, mounting brackets, a socket wrench, and a torque wrench.

Step 1: Prepare Your Vehicle

The first step in installing running boards on your Chevy Silverado is to prepare your vehicle. This includes removing any existing hardware, such as mud flaps or rocker panels, and cleaning the area where you’ll be installing the running boards.

Next, you’ll need to locate the mounting points under your vehicle. This will vary depending on your Chevy Silverado’s make and model, so be sure to consult your owner’s manual or seek professional help if you’re not sure where to look.

Step 2: Install the Mounting Brackets

Once you’ve found the mounting points, it’s time to install the mounting brackets. To do this, simply attach the brackets to the mounting points using the provided hardware and tighten them with your socket wrench.

Be sure to follow the manufacturer’s instructions carefully and make sure the brackets are secure before moving on to the next step.

Step 3: Install the Running Boards

Now that your mounting brackets are in place, it’s time to install the running boards themselves. To do this, simply attach the running boards to the mounting brackets using the provided hardware and tighten them with your torque wrench.

Again, it’s important to follow the manufacturer’s instructions carefully and make sure the running boards are secure before testing them out.

Step 4: Test Out Your New Running Boards

Finally, it’s time to test out your new running boards. Take your Chevy Silverado for a spin and make sure everything is working properly. If you notice any issues or concerns, be sure to contact the manufacturer or seek professional help.

Congratulations! You’ve successfully installed running boards on your Chevy Silverado.
