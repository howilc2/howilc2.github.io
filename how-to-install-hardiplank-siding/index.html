If you’re looking to give your home’s exterior a facelift, one great option to consider is hardiplank siding. This durable and attractive option can transform your home’s appearance and provide long-lasting protection from the elements. However, if you’re not familiar with how to install hardiplank siding, the process can seem intimidating. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know about how to install hardiplank siding so you can feel confident tackling this project.

Pain points of installing hardiplank siding

Before we dive into the specifics of how to install hardiplank siding, let’s touch on some of the challenges you might face with this project. One common pain point is the sheer amount of work involved in removing your old siding before you can install the hardiplank. Additionally, cutting and fitting the hardiplank can be a time-consuming and exacting process, particularly if you have complex angles or a multi-story home.

Answering the target of how to install hardiplank siding

With those challenges in mind, let’s look at the steps involved in how to install hardiplank siding. The first step is to remove your existing siding (if you have any) and prepare your exterior walls by installing sheathing and flashing. From there, you’ll install starter strips, corner posts, and any needed trim before cutting and fitting your hardiplank panels. Finally, you’ll apply caulking and paint to complete the installation.

Main points of how to install hardiplank siding

To recap, the primary steps involved in how to install hardiplank siding include:

  • Removing existing siding and preparing sheathing and flashing
  • Installing starter strips and corner posts
  • Measuring, cutting, and fitting hardiplank panels precisely
  • Applying caulking and paint to finish the installation

Of course, these are oversimplified steps, and there are many details to consider within each one. However, this list should give you a general idea of what’s involved in how to install hardiplank siding.

Tools and materials you’ll need to install hardiplank siding

Before you get started with your hardiplank siding installation project, you’ll need to gather some basic tools and materials. Here are some things to add to your shopping and hardware lists:

- Hardiplank siding panels

  • Starter strips
  • Corner posts
  • Trim pieces
  • Caulking
  • Paint
  • Utility knife
  • Circular saw
  • Tin snips
  • Chalk line

Cutting and fitting your hardiplank siding panels

One of the most significant challenges involved in how to install hardiplank siding is cutting and fitting the panels precisely. Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you tackle this part of the process:

- Always measure twice, cut once to avoid mistakes and waste

  • Use a circular saw with a carbide blade to cut straight lines
  • Use tin snips to cut curved or angled sections
  • Use a chalk line to ensure straight cuts and level panels
  • Leave a small gap (1/8 inch or less) between panels for flexibility and movement

Applying caulking and paint to finish your hardiplank siding installation

Once you’ve properly installed your hardiplank panels, the last step is to apply caulking and paint to give your project a polished finish. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

- Choose a high-quality, paintable caulking and apply it to all seams, corners, and edges

  • Allow the caulking to dry completely (usually 24 hours) before painting
  • While you can use many types of paint, we recommend a 100% acrylic latex paint for the best results
  • Apply two coats of paint, allowing each coat to dry completely before adding the next

While the installation process was definitely challenging, we were able to complete the job in just a few weekends by working diligently and carefully. Taking the time to measure twice and cut once was key to our success, and we were thrilled with the finished product once we applied the paint and caulking.

Common questions about how to install hardiplank siding

Question 1: Can I install hardiplank siding over existing siding?

Answer: While it’s technically possible to install hardiplank siding over existing siding, it’s not generally recommended. It’s essential to prepare your exterior walls with proper sheathing and flashing to ensure your hardiplank siding lasts and protects your home. Additionally, adding hardiplank siding over an existing layer of siding can make your exterior walls thicker, potentially affecting window and door placements and functionality.

Question 2: Do I need to prime my hardiplank siding before painting?

Answer: Yes, you should always prime your hardiplank siding before painting to ensure the paint adhesion is strong and long-lasting. Look for a high-quality exterior primer that’s compatible with your selected paint type and apply it evenly before painting.

Question 3: What type of saw should I use to cut hardiplank siding?

Answer: For long, straight cuts, we recommend using a circular saw with a carbide-tipped blade. For more intricate cuts, you can use tin snips or a jigsaw.

Question 4: Is hardiplank siding flammable?

Answer: While hardiplank siding is made primarily of cement and sand, it does contain some wood fibers. However, it has been extensively tested and is considered non-combustible and flame-resistant. Additionally, it does not release toxic fumes when exposed to heat or flames.

Conclusion of how to install hardiplank siding

Overall, installing hardiplank siding can be a time-consuming and challenging project, but the results are well worth the effort. By following these tips and tricks for how to install hardiplank siding, you can transform your home’s exterior and provide long-lasting, durable protection against the elements. Whether you’re a seasoned DIYer or a newbie to home improvement projects, with a little patience and determination, you can successfully tackle a hardiplank siding installation.
